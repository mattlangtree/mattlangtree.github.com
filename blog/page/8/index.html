Leopard Mount Samba Shares

Aug 5th, 2008

I have created a basic apple script that offers the user feedback about the progress of mounting your network samba shares on Leopard.

To use this script, post it into Script Editor, update the relevant server and share variables.

Click the Run button to test the script out.

Once you are happy with your new script, choose File > Save.. and choose Application as the File Format, and check ‘Run Only’ from the options in the Save dialog box.

MacBook Pro 17” + 4 FREE Surprises!

Jul 28th, 2008

Yesterday I called up apple support asking them if I could choose a non-glossy display for the MacBook Pro (MBP) 17” that I wanted to order from the Mac Refurbished store.

The very helpful lady sincerely apologised and said “the glossy and non-glossy MBPs are all stored in the same spot, and we can’t open them to find out which one we are sending you”.

Despite having to brave the possibility of receiving a glossy display, I went ahead and ordered the MBP 17” rather than taking the risk of ordering from a random seller on eBay.

So, here is the link to the product that I ordered: http://store.apple.com/au/product/FA897X/A

2.4Ghz MBP 17” 1680x1050, 5400rpm 160GB HDD(and possibly a glossy or non- glossy display, it’s completely random)

And here is what I got:

2.6Ghz MBP 17” 1920x1200, 7200rpm 200GB w/ Matte display

As any nerd would attest, getting a

1) CPU upgrade (2.4 ->2.6) 2) display resolution upgrade (1680x1050 -> 1920x1200) 3) HDD capacity (160GB -> 200GB ) 4) HDD speed upgrade (5400rpm ->7200rpm)

is an extremely lucky surprise.

Just as a side note, every one of those 4 upgrades is an added expense on top of the base 17” MBP.

I am an extremely happy chappy today. :-)

Wordpress Releases Feature-rich 2.6 Version of Blogging Software (Updated X2)

Jul 15th, 2008

The new version 2.6 release from the folks at Wordpress comes as a suprise, arriving a month ahead of schedule.

The new blogging software touts the following new features:

  • Post Revisions: Wiki-like tracking of edits - Press This! Button (now extracts youtube video code/flickr source for easy image/video embedding in posts) - Google Gears support: Speed up your blogging experience by caching Javascript/CSS on your local computer - Theme Previews: Preview a theme running on your site - without having the public blog appearance change
    Some other new features, which I’m glad to see are: - Full SSL support - Image Captions - Custom wp-config.php location - for “clean” svn checkouts.

The press release is available here: http://wordpress.org/development/2008/07/wordpress-26-tyner/

Thanks to Adam Wolfs, for the heads up about this new release!

UPDATE: I’ve installed Wordpress 2.6 on this domain, using the SVN installation method. I’m happy to announce that the process for an SVN installation is mostly trivial and the site was only down for about 30 seconds.

UPDATE 2: The admin area does seem to be a little snappier at times, after installing Google gears on Firefox 3.

Flow FTP Updated to 1.0.2

Jul 1st, 2008

The folks over at Extendmac have released the latest instalment (version 1.0.2) of their new Flow FTP Client.

The update includes the following release notes:

Fixes for: - FTP URLs - Editor Crashing - Reload, action, and search toolbar items - (S)FTP Directory listings - Bookmark folder creation - Droplets - Duplication - Deletion - Info View - Source List-related crashes - Setting permissions on upload - Autosaving (backing up) of files in the internal editor. - Concurrency/Threading

Changes to: - "Permissions" section of InfoView is now un-editable, and displayed in symbolic notation instead of an octal. - There is now a growl notification for "File Saved," when editing changes (both internal/external) are saved.

Australian iTunes Store Receives TV Shows

Jun 24th, 2008

In a predicted move from media giant Apple Co., the Australian iTunes store now contains TV shows.

Australian iTunes users are now greeted with a selection from Australian networks, ABC and Nine Network along with US Movie studios ABC Studios, Disney Channel and MTV.

Episodes are priced at $2.99 each and are available through the iTunes software available for Mac and Windows. The TV shows contain DRM (Digital Rights Management) and are able to be played back on computers that have the iTunes software installed.

TV shows can be synched to the latest generation of Apple’s iPod music players including the iPod Nano, iPod Touch and the iPod Classic.

The news of TV shows on the Australian iTunes store coincides with other recent news that Australia is to be one of the first of 20+ countries to receive the new iPhone 3G.

Source: MacRumors.com

Optus iPhone 3G Prices Leaked

Jun 11th, 2008

Gizmodo Australia has published prices that they believe Optus will be charging for the new iPhone 3G in Australia.

Gizmodo claims that Optus Australia will be offering a $79 cap which includes 1GB/month of data and $300 worth of calls and SMS and a $99 cap includes 3GB/month of data and $400 worth of calls and SMS.

Prices for the iPhone 3G haven’t been announced publicly by Optus yet, so we cannot be certain that this is all that Optus will bring to the table.

A websites for registering your interest in the iPhone 3G has been set up by Optus Australia.
